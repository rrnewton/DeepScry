## Legal Sets

The 2010 main event included a **Standard (Type II)** segment (alongside Scars of Mirrodin draft and Extended, which we do not capture). As of the **December 2010** event date (after the October 2010 rotation when Scars of Mirrodin arrived), the Standard legal pool was the **Zendikar block + Scars** plus the current core set:

- **Magic 2011** (M11, Jul 2010) — base/core set
- **Zendikar** (ZEN, Oct 2009)
- **Worldwake** (WWK, Feb 2010)
- **Rise of the Eldrazi** (ROE, Apr 2010)
- **Scars of Mirrodin** (SOM, Oct 2010)

**Boundary note:** The October 2010 rotation dropped **Magic 2010 (M10)** and the entire **Alara block** (Shards of Alara / Conflux / Alara Reborn). **Mirrodin Besieged** (Feb 2011) had not yet released. Jace, the Mind Sculptor (Worldwake) was the format's apex card and would be banned in Standard in February 2011; Primeval Titan (M11) and Stoneforge Mystic (WWK) were also format-warping. This is the **December 2010** event (won by Matignon); the *following* December-2011 championship was a separate event.

## Card Pool Context

The 2010 Worlds was dominated by **UB Control** with Jace, the Mind Sculptor at its apex — Jace would be banned in Standard a few months later in February 2011. Five of the eight Top 8 players ran UB Control shells. Love Janse's Eldrazi Ramp was the notable outlier, leveraging Primeval Titan (also soon banned) and Emrakul. The Matignon–Wafo-Tapa all-French final was historic.

**Year disambiguation:** This is the "2010 World Championship" held in December 2010. The *following year's* championship, held in December 2011 in San Francisco, was won by Jun'ya Iyanaga with R/G Wolf Run. Our target is the **December 2010 event**.
