# Description

## Context
- Date: 2026-04-03
- Puzzle: puzzles/test_trample_excess_damage.pzl
- Seed: 1
- Mode: heuristic-vs-heuristic

## Steps to Reproduce
1. Run: `./target/release/mtg tui --start-state puzzles/test_trample_excess_damage.pzl --p1=heuristic --p2=heuristic --seed=1 --verbosity=3`
2. Observe Turn 1 combat

## Expected Behavior
Carnage Tyrant (7/6, Trample) attacks. Grizzly Bears (2/2) blocks.
Per CR 702.19c, the attacking creature must assign at least lethal damage to the blocker (2 damage), and the excess (5 damage) tramples to the defending player.

Expected result: Grizzly Bears dies (2 damage assigned), Player 2 takes 5 trample damage (5 → 0), Player 2 loses.

## Actual Behavior
```
Combat: Carnage Tyrant (9) (7 damage) ↔ Grizzly Bears (17) (2 damage)
Grizzly Bears (17) goes to graveyard
Grizzly Bears (17) dies from combat damage
```

Player 2 life stays at 5. No trample damage is dealt to the player. ALL 7 damage is absorbed by the 2/2 blocker. The game continues and Player 1 only wins on Turn 3 when attacking with no blockers.

## Rules Notes
- CR 702.19c: "If an attacking creature with trample is blocked, but there are no creatures blocking it when damage is assigned, all its damage is assigned to the player or planeswalker it's attacking."
- CR 702.19b: "The attacking creature with trample deals damage to the blocking creature(s) until the damage equals the blocking creature(s)' toughness. The remaining damage is then dealt to the defending player or planeswalker."
- This is a significant gameplay bug — trample is a core keyword ability and its excess damage mechanic is fundamental to how combat works in MTG.

## Evidence
- Command: `./target/release/mtg tui --start-state puzzles/test_trample_excess_damage.pzl --p1=heuristic --p2=heuristic --seed=1 --verbosity=3`
- Player 2 life before combat: 5
- Player 2 life after combat: 5 (should be 0 or less)
- Grizzly Bears correctly dies, but excess damage vanishes
