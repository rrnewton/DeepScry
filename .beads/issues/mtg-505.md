# Description

Test all behavioral aspects of Fireball in MTG Forge-rs (1994 Old School; decks mtg-559, mtg-561, mtg-562).

Card file: cardsfolder/f/fireball.txt
Card text:
  {X}{R} Sorcery
  This spell costs {1} more to cast for each target beyond the first.
  Fireball deals X damage divided evenly, rounded down, among any number of
  targets.

Findings (2026-05-30_#2530(f7a005ca), compat-wave14-jeskai):

1. [x] Parses as {X}{R} Sorcery.
2. [x] SINGLE-target X damage WORKS. With 5 Mountains: X = 4 (5 mana - {R}),
   deals 4 to the chosen target. Reproducer:

```sh
cat > /tmp/fb.pzl <<P
[metadata]
Name:Fireball X
[state]
turn=2
activeplayer=p0
activephase=MAIN1
p0life=20
p0hand=Fireball
p0library=Mountain; Mountain; Mountain; Mountain; Mountain
p0battlefield=Mountain; Mountain; Mountain; Mountain; Mountain
p1life=20
p1library=Plains; Plains; Plains; Plains; Plains
P
./target/release/mtg tui --start-state /tmp/fb.pzl --p1=fixed --p2=zero --p1-fixed-inputs="cast Fireball;*;*;*;pass" --stop-on-choice=10 --json --seed 42 --verbosity 3
```
   Expected: `→ X = 4` then `Fireball (3) deals 4 damage to Player 2 (life: 12)`
3. [BROKEN] MULTI-target DivideEvenly + RaiseCost-per-extra-target NOT
   implemented (filed as mtg-tyvcn). Driving 2 targets logged
   "→ targeting Grizzly Bears" but then resolved "deals 4 damage to Player 2"
   (the creature target was dropped; no divide; no per-target cost bump).

CARD STATUS: PARTIAL — single-target X-burn works; multi-target divide-evenly
and the per-extra-target cost increase are unimplemented (mtg-tyvcn).
