# Description

RESOLVED (2026-06-09_#3084(f6c4713f), claude/compat-1994-wave3).

Paralyze's third ability now works:
  T:Mode$ Phase | Phase$ Upkeep | ValidPlayer$ Player.EnchantedController
    | TriggerZones$ Battlefield | Execute$ TrigUntap
  SVar:TrigUntap:DB$ Untap | Defined$ Enchanted | UnlessCost$ 4
    | UnlessPayer$ EnchantedController | UnlessSwitched$ True
"At the beginning of the upkeep of enchanted creature's controller, that
player MAY pay {4}. If they do, untap the creature."

All three pieces required by this issue were implemented:

1. ValidPlayer$ Player.EnchantedController support in the phase-trigger parser.
   New Trigger flag `enchanted_controller_turn_only` (core/effects.rs). The
   firing sites (game_loop/steps.rs check_phase_triggers and actions/mod.rs
   check_triggers_for_controller) gate on the HOST creature's controller being
   the active player (card.attached_to -> host.controller == active_player),
   NOT the Aura's controller — so the trigger fires on the cursed player's
   upkeep, not the curser's.

2. Defined$ Enchanted resolution for the Untap target. Resolved per-fire in
   check_triggers_for_controller from the Aura's `attached_to` (a placeholder
   CardId 0 at load time).

3. UnlessCost$ 4 | UnlessPayer$ EnchantedController | UnlessSwitched$ True via
   the EXISTING shared UnlessCostWrapper (loader wrap_with_unless_cost) — NOT a
   re-implementation. switched=true means the inner UntapPermanent runs ONLY
   when the {4} is paid. The payer is resolved to the host's controller. The
   pay/don't-pay decision uses the determinism-safe in-engine UnlessCost
   executor documented under mtg-rpmpg (info-independent: reads only the
   payer's own public mana sources + public attached_to/controller), so it is
   network-safe (no desync risk).

DANGER averted: the implementation does NOT untap unconditionally — if the
controller can't or won't pay {4} the creature stays tapped (the doesn't-untap
lock holds). The naive free-escape failure mode warned about in this issue is
explicitly avoided and covered by the no-pay e2e scenario.

Tests:
- Unit (parser shape): game/actions/tests/effects.rs::test_card_compat_paralyze
  now also asserts the BeginningOfUpkeep trigger is flagged
  enchanted_controller_turn_only and is an UnlessCostWrapper{UntapPermanent}
  with a Mana cost + switched=true.
- E2E (runtime, seed 42): tests/paralyze_pay4_untap_e2e.sh with two puzzles
  (test_puzzles/paralyze_pay4_untap_e2e.pzl,
   test_puzzles/paralyze_cannot_pay4_stays_tapped_e2e.pzl):
  * PAY (P1 has 4 Forests): lock holds in the untap step, then the trigger pays
    {4} and untaps -> Grizzly Bears attacks on turn 4 (Player 1 life 18 -> 16).
  * NO-PAY (P1 has 1 Forest): can't pay {4}, bears stay "locked tapped" on
    turns 4 and 6 and never attack again (Player 1 life stays at 18).

Pre-existing SEPARATE concern (NOT this issue, NOT introduced here): on the
enchanted creature's FIRST untap step after Paralyze's ETB-tap, the
doesn't-untap lock (mtg-529's R:Event$ Untap | Layer$ CantHappen) does not yet
apply (the creature untaps once). Verified identical on clean integration
without this change. Filed/tracked under mtg-529 as a follow-up to the untap
replacement lowering.
